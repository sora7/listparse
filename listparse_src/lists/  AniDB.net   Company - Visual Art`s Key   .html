Key is a Japanese visual novel studio under Visual Art's, known for making dramatic and plot-oriented visual novels. Kanon was Key's debut release, which combined an elaborate storyline, an up-to-date anime-style drawing style, and a musical score which helped to set the mood for the game. Key's second game, Air had a similar if not more complex storyline to Kanon and a more thorough gameplay. Both Kanon and Air were originally produced as adult games, but had non-erotic console ports released for the Dreamcast, PlayStation 2, and PlayStation Portable. As of July 2007, Key has released six games, and has worked in the past with Interchannel, and Prototype for the consumer port releases of previous Key titles. Key has announced their eighth game, Rewrite.

Every soundtrack that is released based from one of Key's titles is on a record label called Key Sounds Label. There are other albums on the label not directly related to the visual novels, such as two albums by Lia and one by Eufonius. The albums on this label have music composed by members from Key, such as Maeda Jun, and Shinji Orito.

Before forming Key, the founding members worked for another visual novel development company called Tactics. At the time of Dousei's production, Tactics' first game, four of Key's original staff worked on the game: Itaru Hinoue as art director, Shinji Orito as musical composer, and Miracle Mikipon and Shinory contributing to the computer graphics. After Dousei, the rest of Key's founding staff joined Tactics and contributed to two more games: Moon., and One: Kagayaku Kisetsu e. After the release of One on May 26, 1998, these developers transferred to Visual Art's where they formed Key; the name Key for the studio was decided by majority rule.

In 2001, Visual Art's created the record label Key Sounds Label (KSL).

While the official company name of Key is Visual Art's/Key, there are some music related credits given to Key. For a brief period of time, Key was used as a label of Visual Art's/Key's to produce and distribute their game music, but that was renamed to KSL (Key Sounds Label) in 2001 and formed into a proper music label. This may or may not be the reason they've still used "Key" for music related credits.
